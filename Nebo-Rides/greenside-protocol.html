Tarmac Logistics

Precision coordination from wheels-down to wheels-rolling.

Tail Sync

We track your aircraft by tail number rather than scheduled arrival time, monitoring real-time ADS-B data to position the vehicle precisely as wheels touch down. This tail-number synchronization eliminates the guesswork of private aviation scheduling, where actual arrival times frequently deviate from filed flight plans. Your chauffeur is authorized and moving into ramp position the moment your aircraft begins its taxi.

FBO Access

Our established relationships with FBO ground crews at Teterboro, Van Nuys, Farnborough, and dozens of other private aviation hubs ensure seamless ramp entry authorization for every Greenside transfer. We pre-coordinate with the FBO operations desk hours before your arrival, confirming vehicle credentials, insurance documentation, and parking assignment. This advance coordination eliminates gate delays and ensures your car is planeside when the door opens.

Zero Friction

The Greenside Protocol eliminates every friction point between your aircraft seat and your vehicle seat, bypassing terminal lobbies, baggage carousels, and FBO waiting areas entirely. Your luggage transfers directly from the aircraft hold to the vehicle trunk by our driver while you step from the aircraft stairs into the rear seat. This zero-friction approach saves 15 to 20 minutes per leg and provides the highest level of privacy and discretion available in private aviation ground transport.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the Greenside Protocol.

Can you get tarmac access at every airport?

Tarmac access is subject to local regulations and airport security (MARSEC) levels, which can change with little notice. We maintain active ramp access credentials at major FBOs including Teterboro, Van Nuys, Farnborough, and Al Maktoum, and will confirm Greenside availability for your specific airport and FBO during the booking process. If tarmac access is temporarily restricted, we position your vehicle at the FBO entrance for the next-fastest transfer possible.

Why do you need the tail number?

The tail number allows us to track your specific aircraft's real-time position via ADS-B data, providing precise arrival timing that eliminates guesswork. FBOs also require the tail number to authorize our vehicle's ramp access and confirm the security clearance for the pickup. Providing the tail number at booking is what enables the seamless, zero-wait tarmac transfer that defines the Greenside Protocol.

Do I still need to show ID?

Yes, even with Greenside service, customs and immigration agents may need to verify passports or ID at the aircraft steps for international arrivals. For domestic flights, identification requirements vary by FBO. Our team will advise you on the specific documentation needed for your arrival airport so there are no surprises during the transfer process.

Can you handle flight crew ground transportation as well?

Absolutely. We regularly provide crew transportation for both corporate flight departments and charter operators, shuttling pilots and cabin crew to their hotels or next assignment location. Crew vehicles can be dispatched simultaneously with the principal's Greenside transfer, or staged separately at the FBO. Many flight departments set up standing crew transport accounts with Nebo Rides for consistent, reliable service at their home base and frequent destinations.

What happens if my flight diverts to a different airport?

Because we monitor your tail number in real time, our dispatch team is often aware of diversions before you land. We immediately begin coordinating vehicle repositioning to your new arrival airport and work with the alternate FBO to secure ramp access. In most cases, we can have a vehicle positioned and ready within 30 to 60 minutes of a diversion, depending on the location and local FBO relationships.
