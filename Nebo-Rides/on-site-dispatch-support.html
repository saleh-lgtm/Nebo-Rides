Ground Control

Professional staff managing every aspect of curbside logistics.

Traffic Control

Our on-site coordinators manage the complete driveway flow, vehicle staging areas, and departure sequencing to prevent gridlock during peak event hours. They work with venue security and local traffic authorities to establish dedicated loading zones, one-way traffic patterns, and VIP express lanes. This professional traffic management ensures that even events with 500+ simultaneous departures move smoothly without delays.

Guest Assistance

Our dispatch staff personally guides each guest to their assigned vehicle using live digital manifests synced with the fleet. They open doors, assist with luggage, and provide clear directions to confused attendees who may not know which car is theirs. For multi-venue events, coordinators manage inter-venue shuttle boarding to ensure every guest reaches the correct destination on time.

Driver Comms

Every coordinator maintains a direct encrypted radio link to the entire fleet, bypassing the lag of cell phone calls or app-based messaging. This real-time communication enables instant rerouting when a vehicle breaks down, a road closes, or the event schedule shifts unexpectedly. Our radio-first approach ensures response times measured in seconds rather than minutes, keeping your event transport running flawlessly.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about on-site dispatch support.

How many coordinators do I need?

We generally recommend one on-site dispatch coordinator for every 50 guests or 3 simultaneous vehicle departures to ensure smooth ground operations. For multi-entrance venues or events with staggered departure times, we conduct a complimentary site survey to determine the optimal staffing ratio. Our event logistics team will provide a detailed staffing proposal based on your venue layout, guest count, and transportation timeline.

What do the coordinators wear?

Our staff wears professional "Event Black" attire as the default standard, blending seamlessly with your event production team and venue personnel. Alternatively, coordinators can wear your branded polo shirts, jackets, or custom uniforms upon request at no additional charge. We recommend sharing branded attire at least 48 hours before the event so our team arrives camera-ready and fully integrated with your brand identity.

Do you offer airport greeters?

Yes, we provide both airport greeters for meet-and-greet arrivals and on-site dispatchers for hotel and venue transportation management. Airport greeters hold custom signage at baggage claim, assist with luggage, and escort guests to their pre-assigned vehicles. This end-to-end ground coordination from airport arrival through event departure ensures a seamless experience for every attendee.

Can coordinators manage multi-venue shuttle operations?

Absolutely. Our dispatch coordinators specialize in managing shuttle loops between multiple venues, hotels, and event spaces simultaneously. Each location receives a dedicated coordinator who tracks shuttle positions via GPS, communicates boarding counts by radio, and adjusts departure intervals based on real-time demand. This multi-venue dispatch capability is essential for conferences, incentive trips, and corporate retreats spread across several locations.

What happens if there is an emergency during the event?

Every on-site dispatch team operates with a pre-approved contingency plan that covers vehicle breakdowns, road closures, medical emergencies, and weather disruptions. Our coordinators maintain direct radio contact with all drivers and can reroute the fleet within minutes. We also keep reserve vehicles on standby at major events, ensuring that any disruption is resolved before guests even notice a change to the transportation schedule.
